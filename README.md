# 📊 Statistics Analysis Notebook

The following notebook walks through fundamental concepts in statistics and demonstrates how to calculate, visualize, and interpret them using Python. It is designed as a practical reference for interviews, projects, and revision.

---

## 🔹 Measures of Central Tendency
This section shows how to compute **mean, median, and mode** for a dataset.  
You’ll learn:
- How each measure captures the "center" of data.
- When mean can be misleading (outliers, skewed data).
- How median and mode provide alternative perspectives.

---

## 🔹 Measures of Dispersion
Here we explore **range, interquartile range (IQR), variance, and standard deviation**.  
You’ll see:
- How dispersion quantifies the spread of data.
- Why variance and standard deviation are more robust than range.
- How IQR helps detect outliers.

---

## 🔹 Distribution & Normality
This part covers:
- Identifying outliers using boxplots and z-scores.
- Testing for normality with Shapiro-Wilk, D’Agostino, and KS tests.
- Understanding skewness and what it tells us about data symmetry.

---

## 🔹 Parametric Assumptions
Before applying statistical tests, the notebook checks:
- **Normality** of data.
- **Equal variances** (homoscedasticity).
- **Linearity** between variables.
- **Multicollinearity** in predictors.

---

## 🔹 Hypothesis Testing
Step-by-step demonstrations of:
- **One-sample t-test**: comparing sample mean to a known value.
- **Two-sample t-test**: comparing means across groups.
- **Chi-square test**: testing categorical distributions.
- **ANOVA**: comparing multiple group means.
- **Z-test**: testing deviations from expected values.
- **Proportion test (A/B testing)**: comparing conversion rates.
